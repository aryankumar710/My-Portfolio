Art Gallery Website

Client: The Future Masters
Role: UX Resarch & Design

The Future Masters is an art gallery dedicated to showcasing modern and contemporary artworks by emerging Indian artists. It serves as a platform to support and promote fresh talent, bringing unique and innovative artistic voices into the spotlight of India‚Äôs evolving art scene

*This project was undertaken while working at DeviceDisk, for our client The Future Masters to design their website.

Introduction

This project was a bit different for me because, unlike other full-site projects, I was only responsible for designing the homepage of The Future Masters art gallery website. Since it was focused on modern and contemporary art by emerging Indian artists, I wanted to make sure the design truly reflected that. To begin, I did a competitive analysis to understand how other art gallery websites present their content‚Äîwhat kind of visuals they use, how they showcase artwork, and how they highlight the artists behind the work. The client‚Äôs main goal was to promote both the paintings and the artists, so I structured the homepage with that in mind.

Homepage

While researching art gallery websites, I noticed that many of them use dark-themed color palettes. These dark themes give the site a classic, timeless feel and create visual depth without being too flashy ‚Äî which really works well in letting the artwork take center stage.

Taking inspiration from this, I chose a dark green theme for the project. Dark green is a muted, neutral-leaning color that doesn‚Äôt compete with the artwork. Instead, it complements both colorful and monochromatic pieces and pairs beautifully with other tones like earthy browns, gold, off-white, and black. It helped maintain a sophisticated aesthetic while keeping the focus on the art itself.

For typography, I went with Cinzel, a serif font that feels refined and culturally rich. Serif fonts are widely favored in the art world for their elegance and timelessness, and Cinzel especially brings a classical Roman touch, making it perfect for a high-end, artistic platform like this one.

Since this website is mainly enquiry-based ‚Äî where users can ask about artworks or paintings ‚Äî I added a fixed enquiry form button on the right side of the screen. This ensures users can reach out at any time while browsing, without having to navigate away from what they‚Äôre viewing.

The homepage starts with a simple banner slider that showcases visuals from the gallery along with a strong, eye-catching headline to attract visitors. Each painting is categorized neatly, making it easy for users to explore based on their interests ‚Äî whether it's modern, abstract, traditional, or contemporary.

To give users a broader view, I added a ‚ÄúCollections‚Äù section that highlights some of the best or featured paintings from different categories. Since the client wanted to focus on promoting the artists as well, I introduced a special ‚ÄúArtist of the Day‚Äù section. This not only showcases one featured artist and their best painting but also encourages other artists to be part of the platform ‚Äî creating a sense of recognition and motivation.

To help the gallery grow its online presence, I added a social media spotlight section, where the best painting from a platform (chosen by the client) is highlighted. It adds a dynamic and current feel to the site.

Lastly, for better user engagement and education, I included a blog section that offers articles on painting techniques, art trends, and inspiration. This adds value to both art lovers and aspiring artists ‚Äî helping to build a creative community and support Indian art.
